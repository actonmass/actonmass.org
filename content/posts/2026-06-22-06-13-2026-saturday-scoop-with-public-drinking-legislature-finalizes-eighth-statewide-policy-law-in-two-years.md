## **State house Scoop**

**State house acts with uncharacteristic urgency to expand World Cup drinking, “fun”**  

You may have seen the headlines: Massachusetts’ state house [finalized a bill this week](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260096/627270277/1728840852?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742) to temporarily relax our state’s strict public drinking laws, in a last-ditch effort to appear more fun to tourists visiting for the World Cup and other events this summer.    

Seriously. I wrote that originally as a joke, but here’s what Senator Julian Cyr had to say in the legislature’s [official press release](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260097/627270278/2033142451?pressReleaseId=1402&utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742) about the bill: “I’ve said for years that Massachusetts has a ‘fun’ problem, so I am delighted to see the Legislature pass this bill to bring some much-needed conviviality to our summer… Let’s show the world that we truly do know how to have a good time in Massachusetts. Cheers!”   

You know what? Mission accepted, Senator Cyr. 

This bill had a relatively short timeline compared to other initiatives that pass through our legislature. It was first filed on [May 11th](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260098/627270279/-1685909799?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742), the press [spotlighted it](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260099/627270280/-423425778?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742) on May 27th, and it [passed](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260100/627270281/797892051?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742) the House and Senate on June 4th and 8th respectively. The pesky public hearing process was [skipped completely](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260101/627270282/-1158423074?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742). The two chambers met to hammer out the differences between their versions on June 8th and the governor signed it just hours later. Within less than a month, it went from idea to Massachusetts law. 

My first reaction? Relief. Usually, when our legislature is acting with uncharacteristic speed, it’s to do something nefarious that they don’t want the public to know about—like last week’s House bill [gutting the legislative audit](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260102/627270283/1291406761?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742). 

My second reaction was, honestly, frustration. As I [said to a reporter](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260103/627270284/-461791258?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742) at MassLive, “it’s remarkable to see what priorities they choose to act quickly on while residents have been begging them for months to take action on housing relief, immigrant protection and municipal funding.” 

**With public drinking, legislature finalizes eighth statewide policy law in two years**

Other policies, including uncontroversial ones, seem caught in the ["inexcusable inertia on Beacon Hill,"](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260116/627270297/-6894068?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742) to quote one senator.

When legislators know that they're going to have their job next year, there is little urgency to get work done in a single session. It also means that legislators are unused to competition, and can avoid taking strong stances that might isolate part of their electorate. Each time you take a legislative action, you risk pissing off somebody. The least controversial thing for legislative leaders to do is to take no action and say you're ["study"](https://click.everyaction.com/k/133260118/627270299/973716777?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImRiZTMxOGQ4LTM1NjctZjExMS04ZmNiLTAwMGQzYTE0YjY0MCIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiYWRtaW5AYWN0b25tYXNzLm9yZyINCn0%3D&hmac=m3RbjqqZNOPrffHmgnB0keHmvWknPqfJxNOG9VfTj8c=&emci=7b16436a-d765-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&emdi=dbe318d8-3567-f111-8fcb-000d3a14b640&ceid=35598742#:~:text=3.%20%E2%80%9CSent%20to%20Study%E2%80%9D%20is%20a%20euphemism%20for%20killing%20a%20bill)-ing an issue.
