## **State House Scoop**

**What’s blocking tenant protection policies in Massachusetts?** 

Earlier this year, polling showed that MA voters think [“housing” is the single biggest issue facing our state government](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042325/562451320/-1077842765?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377)– above any other issue, including inflation and immigration. This result would be unsurprising for anyone who’s spent time reading local news or talking to Bay Staters. Last year, Massachusetts had the [third highest monthly rental costs](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042326/562451321/-1499394818?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) in the country. We also ranked third highest for [median price to buy a home](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042327/562451322/1500555953?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377). Everyone is feeling the pinch.

Unfortunately, the government capable of tackling this crisis is more familiar with bottom rungs: last session, the Massachusetts legislature ranked [50th among the 50 states in effectiveness](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042328/562451323/1736967654?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377). The major housing policy passed last session was a borrowing bill that is [not expected to dramatically increase investment or alleviate rent burdens](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042329/562451324/-1020925930?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377). Meanwhile, [86% of lawmakers in MA](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042330/562451325/1693707780?p1=Article_Inline_Text_Link&utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) are homeowners, a figure that is far from representative of the housing challenges faced by their constituents. In fact, there are [more *landlords* in our legislature than renters](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042331/562451326/1693707780?p1=Article_Inline_Text_Link&utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377). 

What’s more, the real estate industry wields [considerable influence](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042332/562451327/802944520?p1=Article_Inline_Text_Link&utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) (paywall) on Beacon Hill, and is one of the biggest spenders on lobbying in the state. Furthermore, workers in real estate were the [single biggest contributor](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042333/562451328/2135015393?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) to the campaign accounts of Beacon Hill leadership last year. If they’re doing pay-to-play, it seems to be working: their influence has allowed them to [block popular proposals](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042334/562451329/802944520?p1=Article_Inline_Text_Link&utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) (paywall) intended to alleviate the housing crisis. 

To be fair, the legislature has passed some significant housing policies in the last few years. However, a deeper look at those policies suggests that these landmark policies were ones that got explicit approval from the real estate industry– and all stopped short of interfering with moneyed interests’ ability to extract maximum profit from housing. 

For example, the historic [MBTA Communities Act](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042335/562451330/-1888983732?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) requires towns near transit stops to upzone for multifamily housing, but does not provide funding for or even require the construction of housing. Promoted by the [Republican Baker administration](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042336/562451331/-1083514900?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377), it assumed that private developers would come in and take advantage of new zoning, boosting supply and lowering rents in the long run. In fact, [industry news](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042337/562451332/-890398670?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) suggests that the MBTA Communities Act has been a huge boon for local real estate. Don’t get me wrong: new housing is desperately needed and transit-oriented development is smart. But the effects of this legislation will take a while to be felt and do not exactly represent the legislature picking the little guy over the big guy. 

A similar story appears for the recent [ban on tenant-paid broker fees](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042338/562451333/-694615472?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377). Although this fixes an exploitative practice that raised costs for renters at the beginning of a rental agreement, it has no impact on the rest of a rental cycle and is unlikely to lower monthly rental costs. Indeed, the policy was [supported by the Greater Boston Real Estate Board and a major landlords association](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042339/562451334/-1109941436?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377). 

Meanwhile, policies that garner widespread public support but would genuinely challenge real estate profits, such as rent control or a high-value real estate transfer fee, die session after session without a vote. Even limited policies, such as “[home rule](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042340/562451335/968954237?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377)” petitions that would allow more stringent tenant protections within a single municipality [fail repeatedly on Beacon Hill](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042341/562451336/-1792558886?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377). **Moneyed interests given veto power on Beacon Hill– a tale as old as time** 

It’s a pattern we’ve seen in policy-making on Beacon Hill before. A Scoop reader recently reminded me of a graphic that illustrates the blockage that popular policies face on Beacon Hill. You might recognize it from an Act on Mass workshop. It’s from a report completed in 2021 by a policy center at Brown University, entitled [“Who’s Delaying Climate Action in Massachusetts?”](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042343/562451338/-529805923?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) 

![](/img/screenshot-2025-08-09-at-3.46.48 pm.png)

The (admittedly complex) graphic shows the fate of climate legislation in Massachusetts, between 2013 and 2018. On the left is the quantity of climate bills introduced. The colors indicate whether the utilities industry was lobbying against the bill (red) or in support (green). The rightward flows represent their passage– or not– through the legislative process. A key detail: during the same period, public testimony on these bills [favored their passage by about 90%](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042344/562451339/-529805923?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377). 

First takeaway: it is remarkable how few bills make it through the legislative process, illustrating the importance of transparency in committees. Of 245 climate and clean energy bills introduced during this time period, the study found that [only 9 made it to a vote by the full legislature](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042345/562451340/-529805923?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377)– despite their popularity with the public. At a time where committee votes were not published, this meant that 96% of bills never got a public vote. Getting public committee votes with the [new rules](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042346/562451341/367287927?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377) means that we the people can finally get a glimpse at what’s happening when the bills we support die in committee.  

Secondly, most of the bills that *did* make it out were those few which had the support of the utilities lobby. I’ll quote [their findings](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042347/562451342/-529805923?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377): “Bills that utilities supported were far more likely to make it into the powerful Ways and Means committees, and then eventually be enacted into law. Similarly, bills that utilities opposed were far less likely to move forward, and **utilities’ preferences had a far higher correlation with outcomes than did any other coalitions**.” 

So, how does that relate to the push for tenant protections in Massachusetts? Well, it seems that a similar dynamic is at play: 

1. Sparse policy action, despite the crisis and the public support for swift action. 
2. The policy action we do get happens to be supported by powerful moneyed interests with access to leadership.
3. Meanwhile, policies opposed by moneyed interests die without a vote.
4. Rinse, repeat. 

Seeing this pattern time and again in the legislature is one reason that Act on Mass has decided to take on Beacon Hill’s power structures directly with our ballot question. Currently, decision-making power is [concentrated in very few hands](https://click.everyaction.com/k/113042348/562451343/-1610353296?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogIjgxYWNiNmEyLTYyNzUtZjAxMS04ZGM5LTYwNDViZGE5ZDk2YiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAic2NvdGlhLmhpbGxlQGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EWvvmKdLubeXtWeG1XjjFNeJkYm7TQpXO9OMYSDf0yk=&emci=83b3a4a7-5f75-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&emdi=81acb6a2-6275-f011-8dc9-6045bda9d96b&ceid=15186377), with rank-and-file lawmakers discouraged from actively participating in the legislative process or making up their own minds on legislation. This empowers moneyed interests, who can use their abundant time and resources to target a few key members of leadership and heavily influence legislation. Meanwhile, everyday citizens have to resort to the ballot to have our voices heard. 

With your help, we can make sure that ends in 2026!
