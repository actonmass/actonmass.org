## **State House Scoop**

This week, followers of Massachusetts politics from Beacon Hill to the Berkshires opened our morning emails to shocking news. News outlet POLITICO announced the abrupt cancellation of its popular daily [“Massachusetts Playbook”](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872164/603213557/-1010973921?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) newsletter, with its head reporter Kelly Garrity departing to the *Boston Globe*. POLITICO did not offer an explanation for the closure but reassured readers that they “will continue covering politics and policy in Washington and elsewhere.”  

Though it is not the only daily newsletter available to those with an interest in Massachusetts politics, the POLITICO newsletter was certainly one of the most well-read and will be missed. Its mix of state house news and reporting on Massachusetts’ delegation in Washington was unique and news about elections or political scandals was often first broken in POLITICO. It was well-read by political insiders and novices alike, spotlighting reporting from newspapers around the state for a wide audience. 

**Closure of POLITICO #MApoli desk mirrors national trends as billionaires consolidate control of news** 

This news unfortunately mirrors press-related stories nationwide. Just this month, *The Washington Post* [laid off 300 reporters](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872165/603213558/1548147008?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103)—a third of its total newsroom—including [most of its local reporters covering the D.C. region](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872166/603213559/600145967?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103). It was the latest in a series of [disturbing management decisions](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872167/603213560/473829408?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) by the *Post*’s multibillionaire owner Jeff Bezos, who has enough money to cover the paper’s annual losses for the next, uh, 2470 years. Meanwhile, legacy news outlet *CBS News* has [lost staff and credibility](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872168/603213561/-940850433?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) as new owner, billionaire heir David Ellison, cozies up to Trump and narrows news coverage, including a recent blocked [interview by Stephen Colbert](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872169/603213562/2045365351?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103). Big takeaway: when billionaires run the game, reporting suffers. 

Although we don’t know what’s behind POLITICO’s decision to end their Massachusetts coverage, it fits this unfortunately familiar pattern. In 2021, POLITICO was bought by German media conglomerate Axel Springer for $1 billion. *Foreign Policy* [reported](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872170/603213563/808011044?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) at the time that the company had a “decades-long record of bending journalistic ethics for right-wing causes.” In January of this year, POLITICO announced its [first-ever layoffs](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872171/603213564/1410949907?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103), in conjunction with a [shifting reporting focus](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872172/603213565/-1170231727?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103)towards markets and finance for “investment professionals.” 

An [extensive academic study](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872173/603213566/1418686889?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) on this phenomenon found that the acquisition of a newspaper by an “investment owner” resulted in a shrinking of the paper’s staff by nine reporters or editors on average. When editorial decisions are driven by profit margins and the interests of billionaire investors, the quality and consistency of reporting declines, leaving behind [“news deserts.”](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872174/603213567/2017570758?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) Back to our takeaway: when billionaires run the game, reporting suffers. 

But why are we talking about this in a Saturday Scoop? 

Well, in our fight for transparency, accountability, and democracy in Massachusetts, the press is one of our most essential allies. This dates back to our country’s founding: Thomas Jefferson argued in favor of the people’s right to “full information of \[the government’s] affairs thro’ the channel of the public papers,”[famously saying](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872175/603213568/1856469173?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103), “were it left to me to decide whether we should have a government without newspapers or newspapers without a government, I should not hesitate a moment to prefer the latter.” 

Whether it’s reporters asking [tough questions](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872176/603213569/1429451871?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103#:~:text=Asked%20by%20reporters%20how%20the%20state%20would%20enforce%20its%20ban%20on%20ICE%20agents%20in%20courts%2C%20on%20state%20and%20private%20property%2C%20Healey%20said%20she%20hoped%20the%20state%20law%20would%20serve%20as%20a%20deterrent.) of elected officials in press conferences, undertaking [deep investigations](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872177/603213570/1862363047?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) to expose corruption, or simply keeping us up-to-date every day, local journalism is one of the most important tools of transparency and accountability we have. 

**For transparency and accountability, we need strong local state house coverage in Massachusetts** 

The decline in regular state house reporting in Massachusetts is part and parcel of the story of declining transparency and efficiency at our state house. Many local newspapers in MA [used to have full-time state house reporters](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872178/603213571/-103181937?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103#:~:text=%E2%80%9CWay%20back%20in%20the%20day%2C%2035%20years%20ago%2C%20a%20lot%20of%20the%20small%20papers%20had%20reporters%20in%20the%20statehouse%2C%E2%80%9D) who reported specifically on the actions of their delegations at Beacon Hill, adding additional scrutiny for electeds and keeping citizens informed. 

Yet, like most areas of the nation, Massachusetts has seen a [shrinking number](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872179/603213572/-766665389?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) of local newspapers, driven by a shift to digital media and increasingly corporate governing structures. Many newspapers across the country were snapped up by the publishing hedge fund, Gannett, which [ended print publication](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872180/603213573/1515720704?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) for 19 Massachusetts newspapers simultaneously in 2022. In [reporting by WGBH](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872181/603213574/2017570758?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) on the decline of local papers, one expert said: "\[when] a newspaper goes out of business, corruption goes up, fines go up, the government has to monitor these things to a greater degree, there's a cost to society.” 

Indeed, as local newspapers closed or reduced coverage, the number of desks filled in our state house’s press gallery has [continued to dwindle](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872182/603213575/-1384843185?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103). As of 2022, Massachusetts had just [17 full-time state house reporters](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872183/603213576/37609407?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103), a number smaller than some states with *part-time* legislatures. The shrinking coverage has left readers dependent on larger publications like POLITICO for regular updates, leaving vulnerabilities when managerial decisions are made in corporate boardrooms far away from the state. And of course, with 200 full-time legislators and a governor’s office serving a population of over 7 million, those 17 reporters have their work cut out for them.

## **Lily's Lowdown**

**Speed cameras: public safety breakthrough or surveillance nightmare?**

After being stalled by legislative inertia for years, Massachusetts state leadership is picking up the threads on legislation to [allow road cameras to catch speeding cars](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872205/603213598/1014626119?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) on Massachusetts roads. 

Although road cameras are [“ubiquitous”](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872206/603213599/1014626119?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) in Massachusetts and regularly used by police for crime solving, state law currently bans their use for speed enforcement. Legislation to address this issue has repeatedly died on Beacon Hill. Taking a new tact, Governor Healey’s new FY2027 budget proposal ([discussed last week](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872207/603213600/-1236201153?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103)) includes a provision to allow cities and towns to install speed cameras in school and construction zones. 

Another bill to address this is [S.2344](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872208/603213601/816754345?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103)/[H.3754](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872209/603213602/805863438?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) , *An Act Relative to Traffic Regulation Using Road Safety Cameras*, was introduced by ranking members of the Joint Committee on Transportation Senator William Brownsberger (Belmont) and Representative Steven Owens of Watertown last year. The [Senate version](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872210/603213603/816754345?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) was passed and has sat in Senate Ways & Means since July. The [House version](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872211/603213604/805863438?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) still has not received a vote. 

In 27 states, radar cameras track speeding vehicles and issue speeding tickets in the absence of a police officer. Advocates have been pushing for the instatement of speed-tracking cameras for years, particularly as [fatal collisions continue to rise](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872212/603213605/-1353189351?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) and Massachusetts cities remain [some of the most collision-prone cities](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872218/603213609/-799667728?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) in the country. 

The cameras’ sensors detect speeds exceeding the posted limit by 11 miles per hour or more. The speeding vehicle is then captured either via photograph or video, which is sent to the jurisdiction to be evaluated by law enforcement, who issue the violation. The practice, in theory, would reduce the burden of traffic enforcement on local police and make communities safer from reckless driving.

But the issue is layered, and fraught with data privacy concerns. Part of the reason using the cameras for speed-tracking makes sense, Rep. Owens reasons, is because the infrastructure for it already exists; across Massachusetts, police departments use automatic license plate recognition (ALPR) [cameras to track license plates in a national database](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872220/603213613/-100064849?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103). Most of these AI-powered cameras are sourced from Flock Safety, a security hardware company. Although the company does not contract with ICE, Flock has faced heightened scrutiny because the data it captures with its cameras can be [shared with ICE by local law enforcement. ](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872226/603213618/-1311513797?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103)

Somewhat promising is the language included in S.2344/ H.3754 that would ostensibly protect against data sharing from speed cameras with agencies like ICE. Below is language from the bill’s [committee summary](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872230/603213622/805863438?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103):

"Specifies that cameras shall not capture images unless a violation occurs and that such images are to be destroyed within 48 hours after the final disposition of a camera enforceable violation... Stipulates that photos and other personally identifiable information are not public records. Prohibits the camera system from capturing a front facing photo of the vehicle or its occupants, noting however, that no such photo shall be dismissed solely on the basis that it captures the vehicle’s occupants or contents."

The legislation, if passed, would also require municipalities to carry out a “public awareness campaign” to inform people of the speed camera installation. 

Advocates also worry that the technology could be weaponized against minority communities that already suffer overpolicing [in Boston and across Massachusetts.](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872233/603213628/-1044960777?p1=Article_Feed_ContentQuery&p1=Article_Feed_ContentQuery&utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103) Healey’s [budget proposal, ](https://click.everyaction.com/k/125872234/603213629/-177758941?utm_medium=&nvep=ew0KICAiVGVuYW50VXJpIjogIm5ncHZhbjovL3Zhbi9FQS9FQTAwNy8xLzkwMTUxIiwNCiAgIkRpc3RyaWJ1dGlvblVuaXF1ZUlkIjogImFiNWIxOTJlLThlMGYtZjExMS1hNjlhLTAwMGQzYTU3NTkzZiIsDQogICJFbWFpbEFkZHJlc3MiOiAiZG9jbGl6OTk5QGdtYWlsLmNvbSINCn0%3D&hmac=EX-bUJWC31AgljHawk81USqpCS4dtia-AxN8XiGsDqg=&emci=8ac4dfee-830e-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&emdi=ab5b192e-8e0f-f111-a69a-000d3a57593f&ceid=33585103)which is separate from the proposed legislation, does not include a requirement to report on racialized policing that may arise from use of the cameras. 

Still, the disconnect between the security apparatus of radar cameras all over the state and the fact that they are not used to discipline speeding drivers feels intuitively wrong. Without clear, specific measures, however, that bar local police departments—particularly those with 287(g) (information-sharing) agreements with ICE—it’s not hard to see how speed cameras might be deployed as a weapon of mass-surveillance.
